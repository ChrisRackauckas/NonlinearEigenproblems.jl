Tutorial: Method of fundamental solutions

Suppose we want to solve the following Helmholtz eigenvalue problem

\[\begin{cases} (\Delta +\lambda^2)u(x)=0&,&\;\; x\in\Omega\\ u(x)=0&,&x\in\Gamma \end{cases}\]

Moreover, we suppose that that we have source points at $s_1,\ldots,s_N \not\in\Omega$. The fundamental solution of Helmholtz equation is defined by the relation

\[-(\Delta +\lambda^2)G_k(x,s)=\dirac(x,s).\]

where $s$ is a source point. The fundamental solution in three dimensions is

\[G_\lambda(x,s)=\frac{1}{4\pi|x-s|}e^{-iλ|x-s|}.\]

With the method of fundamental solution we assume the solution can be approximated by

\[u(x)=\alpha_1 G_\lambda(x,s_1)+\cdots+\alpha_N G_\lambda(x,s_N).\]

where $\alpha_j$ is the intensity of the source point $s_j$.
